Homemade

Nothing will ever beat the feeling of having a good home-cooked meal. especially with it being something that you grew up eating. Throughout my childhood, my mom was always whipping up something delicious in the kitchen. It is especially nostalgic when she makes something I have not eaten in a while.

Being surrounded by Lao culture, food is one of the most important ways for me to stay connected to my roots. Especially with authentic Lao dishes, ordering at a restaurant is never the same as having it homemade.

Despite that, this is one of the closest recipes I have been able to find to make a Lao-style coconut jelly, a dessert near and dear to my heart.

Foods I Have Tried
Seafood Boil Lao-style Brisket Khao Poon Nam Seen
This is a cajun-style seafood boil with shrimp and lobster. The sauce was seasoned to perfection and had a spicy kick that you cannot miss in a seafood boil. This is a homemade Lao brisket dish, deliciously paired with Asian steamed ham, and two of the best Lao dipping sauces. The sauces are extremely savory and spicy, with each using lime juice, garlic, and chili as the base flavors. Khao Poon is another Laotian dish that is one of my favorites. The soup base tastes slightly sour, with coconut milk as the main ingredient. The noodles are rice vermicelli noodles, and the toppings include: shredded cabbage, beef brisket, green beans, pork blood, mint leaves, etc.
Laab Seen and Thum Muk Thang Pizza Apple Pie
Laab seen is, without a doubt, my favorite Lao dish to exist. It is a spicy beef salad with mint, bean sprouts, liver, This is another pizza, but this one is homemade to perfection! The fluffy crust combined with the custom toppings--jalapenos, onions, pineapple-- made for an amazing dinner. This apple pie took hours of preparation, but it was well-worth all the time put into it! The crust was incredibly buttery and the cinnamon crumble on top added the extra taste of fall.
